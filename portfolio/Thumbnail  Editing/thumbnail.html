Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services.

A revision is a modification made to your thumbnail based on your feedback after the first design is delivered. Revisions may include changes to text, colors, images, positioning, effects, or other design elements. Revisions do not include creating a completely new thumbnail concept unless discussed separately.

A revision is a request to make changes to the edited video after the first version is delivered. Examples include changing text, music, transitions, colors, effects, captions, timing, or other editing elements. Revisions help ensure the final video matches your requirements and expectations. Major changes that require re-editing large portions of the project may be treated as additional work.

The number of revisions depends on the package you select. Additional revisions can be purchased as an add-on.

Editable PSD source files are included with Premium packages. They can also be purchased separately as an add-on.

An Editable PSD Source File is the original Photoshop project file used to create your design. It contains all layers, text, effects, and elements, allowing you or another designer to make future modifications without starting from scratch.
